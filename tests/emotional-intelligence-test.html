Emotional Intelligence Test

Ten questions about how you typically notice and handle emotions — your own and other people's. This covers three common themes: self-awareness, empathy, and staying steady under pressure. Answer based on how you usually actually act, not your best moment or your worst one. You'll get a single 0-100 score along with a short note on what that range tends to mean.

This is an informal self-check, not a validated psychometric or clinical tool.

How it's measured

Each answer is worth 0 to 3 points, from "Rarely" to "Almost always." We add up all ten answers and scale the total to a 0-100 score. A higher score means the listed behaviors show up more often for you — it's a snapshot of tendencies, not a fixed trait or a comparison against other people.

Frequently asked questions

Is this a validated EQ test?

No — it's an informal, original self-check inspired by common emotional-intelligence themes, not a validated psychometric instrument.

Can emotional intelligence improve over time?

Most researchers in this area think so — skills like noticing your own reactions or staying calm under stress tend to respond to practice and reflection, unlike IQ.
