Brain Dominance Test

"Left brain, right brain" is more pop-psychology metaphor than literal neuroscience — but the underlying question it points at is real: do you naturally lean toward structured, analytical thinking, or toward open-ended, creative thinking, when you're solving a problem? This eight-question self-assessment gives you a simple read on that lean, expressed as a score split between "Analytical" and "Creative" rather than a single rigid label.

How it's measured

Each of the eight questions offers two options — one reflecting a more analytical approach, one reflecting a more creative approach. Every answer adds one point to that side; your final result shows the split between the two, so a near-even score is reported honestly as a near-even split rather than forced into one category.

Frequently asked questions

Is "left brain / right brain" scientifically accurate?

Not literally — most cognitive tasks engage both hemispheres together. The phrase survives as shorthand for analytical-versus-creative tendencies, which is how we use it here: as a metaphor, not a brain scan.

Can I score evenly between both?

Yes, and plenty of people do — the result shows your exact split rather than forcing a single label.
